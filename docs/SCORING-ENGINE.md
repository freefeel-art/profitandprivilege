# Scoring Engine

## Purpose

Transform evaluation metrics into a final Opportunity Score.

The scoring engine must be transparent, explainable and reproducible.

---

## Evaluation Categories

1. Competition Score
2. Startup Difficulty
3. Fulfillment Difficulty
4. Time To Revenue
5. Economic Potential
6. Distribution Difficulty
7. Testability

Range:

1 - 10

---

## Weighting

Competition Score:
10%

Startup Difficulty:
10%

Fulfillment Difficulty:
15%

Time To Revenue:
10%

Economic Potential:
30%

Distribution Difficulty:
15%

Testability:
10%

Total:
100%

---

## Weighting Philosophy

Economic Potential receives the highest weighting.

Reason:

The purpose of the framework is to identify valuable long-term opportunities rather than short-term wins.

Businesses with:

- recurring revenue
- scalability
- asset value
- leverage
- exit potential

should outperform businesses that simply generate fast cash.

---

## Opportunity Score

Range:

1 - 100

Formula:

Weighted average of all evaluation categories.

Purpose:

Provide a single summary score.

The score is a guide.

The score is never the decision.

---

## Score Interpretation

90 - 100

Exceptional Opportunity

Rare combination of:

- strong economics
- scalability
- defensibility
- practical execution

---

75 - 89

Strong Opportunity

Worth serious consideration.

---

60 - 74

Viable Opportunity

Potentially attractive depending on circumstances.

---

40 - 59

Weak Opportunity

Requires caution.

Significant weaknesses exist.

---

Below 40

Poor Opportunity

Likely unsuitable.

Requires exceptional circumstances to justify pursuit.

---

## Display Rules

Users must always see:

- Opportunity Score
- Individual category scores
- Strengths
- Risks
- Recommended actions

The Opportunity Score alone is insufficient.

---

## Design Principle

The framework should favor:

- scalable businesses
- recurring revenue
- asset creation
- leverage

The framework should not automatically favor:

- quick wins
- easy startups
- low-barrier opportunities
