# First User Tests — Opportunity Validator v0.2

Version: 1.0
Date: 2026-06-16
Target: 10 completed sessions
Reference: VALIDATION-PLAN.md

---

## Who To Test With

Find people who have seriously considered starting something in the past 12 months. They do not need to have launched. They need to have evaluated options.

Good fits:

- Someone currently employed who is thinking about a side income
- Someone who recently left a job and is weighing what to build next
- A freelancer or consultant considering a productised offering
- A developer or creator who has evaluated SaaS or content models
- A small business owner who has looked at adjacent opportunities

Avoid:

- People who have never considered starting or owning something
- People who would feel obligated to say positive things
- Anyone who has seen the scoring framework or been involved in this project

Reach: personal network first. LinkedIn second. No cold outreach for the first 10.

---

## How To Run Each Session

Send the URL. Say nothing else about what the tool does.

Message to send:

> I'm testing something I built. Would you be willing to spend 10 minutes with it and tell me what you think? No setup, just a link. Honest reactions are more useful than positive ones.

After they have used it, ask the debrief questions below. Async (written) is fine. A short call is better.

Do not explain the scoring framework before or during the session. Explanation after is fine.

---

## Questions To Ask

Ask all 7. Keep them in order. Do not prompt or lead.

**1.** What opportunity did you evaluate, and does the score feel right to you?

**2.** Which category score surprised you — and was the surprise because it was too high, too low, or because you didn't expect it to matter?

**3.** Did the Critical Risks section name anything you hadn't already considered?

**4.** Read the Recommended Actions. Which one would you do first, if any?

**5.** Did reading this change or sharpen how you think about the opportunity? If yes, how?

**6.** If you were seriously considering this opportunity tomorrow, would you use this tool before deciding?

**7.** What is the one thing most missing from this evaluation?

---

## What Feedback To Collect

For each session, record:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Tester # | 1 through 10 |
| Opportunity evaluated | Name from the registry |
| Score reaction | Trusted / Questioned / Disputed |
| Category that surprised them | Which one and direction |
| Risk they hadn't considered | Yes / No / Already knew all |
| Action they would take first | Verbatim if possible |
| Did it change their thinking | Yes / No / Somewhat |
| Would use before a real decision | Yes / No / Maybe |
| What is missing | Verbatim — do not paraphrase |

Collect responses in a single shared document or spreadsheet. Do not aggregate until all 10 are complete.

---

## Signals That Indicate Success

Stop and note these when they appear:

- Tester evaluates a second opportunity without being asked
- Tester says something like "I didn't think about it that way"
- Tester asks whether a specific opportunity not in the list can be added
- Tester shares the link with someone else unprompted
- Tester says the score matched their intuition but the report added detail they lacked
- A Recommended Action is described as the first thing they would actually do
- Tester references the tool in a follow-up conversation days later

Any one of these is a meaningful signal. Three or more across 10 sessions is a strong result.

---

## Signals That Indicate Confusion

Stop and note these when they appear:

- Tester asks what the numbers mean before finishing the report
- Tester cannot say which section was most useful when asked
- Tester says the Recommended Actions could apply to anything
- Tester disputes a score but cannot explain why — indicates a calibration problem, not a presentation problem
- Tester reads the Opportunity Score and immediately closes the tab
- Tester says the output felt the same across two different opportunities
- Tester asks "what am I supposed to do with this?"

Any confusion signal from more than two testers on the same point is a pattern. Treat it as a finding, not an outlier.

---

## After 10 Sessions

1. Review all verbatim responses to question 7 (what is missing). Group by theme.
2. Count success and confusion signals. Map against VALIDATION-PLAN.md criteria.
3. Identify any category score disputed by two or more testers independently.
4. Write a one-paragraph summary: what passed, what failed, what was missing most often.
5. Apply the decision framework in VALIDATION-PLAN.md.

Do not act on individual feedback. Act on patterns.
