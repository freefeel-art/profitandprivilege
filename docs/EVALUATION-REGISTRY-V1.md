# Evaluation Registry V1

## Purpose

Define category scores for the MVP opportunity registry.

Scores are authored using Framework v3 principles.

Each score is accompanied by a one-sentence justification.

---

## Scoring Reference

All categories scored 1 to 10.

High score is always favorable.

- Competition Score: 10 = limited competition. 1 = crowded market.
- Startup Difficulty: 10 = easy to launch. 1 = difficult to launch.
- Fulfillment Difficulty: 10 = easy to deliver. 1 = operationally demanding.
- Time To Revenue: 10 = fast revenue. 1 = very slow revenue.
- Economic Potential: 10 = strong long-term value. 1 = weak economic ceiling.
- Distribution Difficulty: 10 = easy to reach customers. 1 = difficult acquisition.
- Testability: 10 = fast and cheap to validate. 1 = slow and expensive.

## Opportunity: Micro SaaS

Source: OPPORTUNITY-REPORT-SPEC.md

Competition Score: 4

Most niches have established players and the barrier to search and switch
is low for buyers.

Startup Difficulty: 3

Requires sustained technical development, product architecture, and iteration
before anything can be sold.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 8

Software delivers to every customer simultaneously with no proportional increase
in labor.

Time To Revenue: 4

Building a functional product takes months before a first paying customer
is realistic.

Economic Potential: 10

Recurring revenue, high margins, scalability, and strong exit potential make
this the highest economic ceiling of any solo opportunity type.

Distribution Difficulty: 4

Customer acquisition for SaaS requires content, paid channels, or communities,
none of which produce fast or cheap results.

Testability: 5

A landing page can validate interest but a working product is required to
validate retention, which takes significant time.

## Opportunity: AI Agency

Competition Score: 3

The market has rapidly saturated as AI tooling became accessible to anyone
with basic technical knowledge.

Startup Difficulty: 7

No product to build and no capital required — a working knowledge of AI
tools and a first outreach effort are sufficient to launch.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 4

Client engagements are bespoke, time-intensive, and difficult to systematize
without significant operational investment.

Time To Revenue: 8

A first paying client can be acquired within days or weeks through direct
outreach without a long sales cycle.

Economic Potential: 5

Revenue is constrained by hours available unless the model transitions to
productized services or software, which is a different business.

Distribution Difficulty: 5

Cold outreach and LinkedIn prospecting are accessible but require consistent
effort with no compounding return.

Testability: 8

A single pilot engagement confirms whether the service delivers value at
minimal cost to validate the business.

## Opportunity: Affiliate Marketing

Competition Score: 3

Most profitable niches are dominated by authoritative sites with years of
content, backlinks, and established audience trust.

Startup Difficulty: 8

No product, no inventory, and no capital are required — signing up for
affiliate programs and publishing content is sufficient to begin.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 9

Product delivery is entirely the merchant's responsibility, leaving the
affiliate with no operational burden after the referral.

Time To Revenue: 4

Building organic traffic sufficient to generate meaningful commissions
typically requires six to eighteen months of consistent content output.

Economic Potential: 7

Content assets scale without proportional labor, carry demonstrable asset
value, and exit through a liquid secondary market at 30-40x monthly earnings.

Distribution Difficulty: 3

Organic search is slow and competitive, and paid traffic typically erodes
margins in affiliate models.

Testability: 5

A single piece of content can be published quickly, but validating whether
the traffic and conversion model works requires months of data.

## Opportunity: Consulting

Competition Score: 5

Competition exists but is partially neutralized by expertise, credentials,
and professional network, which create natural differentiation.

Startup Difficulty: 9

A consulting practice requires only existing expertise and the willingness
to make the first sales conversation.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 3

Every client engagement requires active participation from the consultant,
making this model extremely difficult to scale beyond one or two practitioners.

Time To Revenue: 8

A first client can be contracted within days through a network referral or
direct outreach.

Economic Potential: 5

Revenue is bounded by available hours and a consulting practice has
limited asset value or exit potential in most cases.

Distribution Difficulty: 6

Professional networks and referrals are relatively accessible channels that
do not require significant marketing investment.

Testability: 9

The first sales conversation is itself a validation experiment — rejection
or acceptance provides immediate market signal.

## Opportunity: E-commerce

Competition Score: 2

Almost every product category is dominated by Amazon, large brands, or
entrenched direct-to-consumer competitors with substantial marketing budgets.

Startup Difficulty: 5

Launching requires product sourcing or manufacturing, inventory procurement,
store setup, and initial working capital.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 3

Physical order fulfillment, inventory management, shipping, returns, and
customer service create a persistent operational burden at every scale.

Time To Revenue: 6

A store can go live in weeks, but meaningful revenue depends on traffic
acquisition which adds time before real returns are seen.

Economic Potential: 6

A brand with a differentiated product can scale, carry asset value, and
achieve an exit, but margins are structurally thinner than software.

Distribution Difficulty: 3

Paid acquisition costs are high and competitive, SEO is slow, and
marketplaces extract significant margin from sellers.

Testability: 6

Dropshipping or a small initial batch allows market testing before
committing to significant inventory investment.

## Opportunity: Local Business

Competition Score: 5

Geographic boundaries limit the competitive surface, and established local
businesses rarely compete aggressively for the same customer.

Startup Difficulty: 5

Requirements vary significantly by type but most local service businesses
require either specific skills, equipment, or licensing before launch.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 4

Delivery requires physical presence and direct labor, which creates a hard
ceiling on how many customers can be served.

Time To Revenue: 8

A first paying customer can typically be acquired within days through
local directories, referrals, or community outreach.

Economic Potential: 6

Recurring service contracts, regional scalability, and an active private
equity acquisition market for trades businesses create real long-term value.

Distribution Difficulty: 7

Local SEO, Google Business Profile, and word of mouth are accessible
channels that do not require large marketing budgets.

Testability: 8

First-job feedback is immediate and provides clear signal on whether
the service delivers enough value to generate repeat business.

## Opportunity: Network Marketing

Competition Score: 3

Most warm markets are exhausted or skeptical, and trust in the model has
declined substantially across general consumer audiences.

Startup Difficulty: 8

Entry requires only a starter kit purchase and a company enrollment, with
no technical skills or business infrastructure needed.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 7

Products and logistics are handled by the parent company, limiting the
distributor's operational burden to sales and recruiting activity.

Time To Revenue: 6

A first sale can happen quickly to a warm contact, but building meaningful
recurring income requires team development over many months.

Economic Potential: 3

The structural reality is that a very small percentage of participants
generate significant income, and the model creates no independent asset value.

Distribution Difficulty: 4

Reliance on personal networks means the addressable audience is finite
and depletes quickly without systematic prospecting outside warm contacts.

Testability: 7

Personal sales ability and product reception can be tested quickly
with a small initial outreach effort to existing contacts.

## Opportunity: YouTube Channel

Competition Score: 3

YouTube is a mature platform where most content categories have established
creators with years of content, subscriber bases, and algorithm favor.

Startup Difficulty: 7

Production can begin with a smartphone and free editing software, requiring
no capital and minimal technical setup.

Fulfillment Difficulty: 8

Published content delivers to unlimited viewers simultaneously with
no additional work required after upload.

Time To Revenue: 2

YouTube's Partner Program requires 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours,
which most new channels take twelve to twenty-four months to achieve.

Economic Potential: 7

A successful channel generates advertising revenue, sponsorships, product
sales, and community income, and the audience itself is a compounding asset.

Distribution Difficulty: 4

Algorithm discoverability is highly competitive, and growing a new channel
requires consistent output over an extended period before search traction builds.

Testability: 5

A video can be published immediately, but whether the content concept
and channel positioning will build an audience takes months to determine.
